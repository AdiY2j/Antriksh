Ganymede

Ganymede is the largest satellite in our solar system. It is larger than Mercury and Pluto, and three-quarters the size of Mars.

10 Facts to Know About Ganymede

1

BIGGEST MOON

Ganymede is the largest moon in our solar system. It’s even bigger than Mercury, and the dwarf planet Pluto.

2

TWO GIANTS

Ganymede, the solar system’s largest moon, orbits Jupiter, the solar system’s largest planet.

3

LONG DAYS

Ganymede's day is just over seven Earth days long, the same amount of time it takes Ganymede to orbit Jupiter once.

4

ROCK SOLID

Ganymede has a solid surface made of ice mixed with rock.

5

THIN ATMOSPHERE

There’s evidence Ganymede has a faint oxygen atmosphere. It would still be far too thin to breathe, though.

6

UNDERGROUND OCEAN

Ganymede’s subterranean ocean could have more water than all the water on Earth's surface. (Jupiter’s moon Europa may have an even bigger global ocean with twice as much liquid water as Earth’s ocean.)

7

IT'S MAGNETIC

The Galileo spacecraft discovered that Ganymede has its own magnetosphere – a region of charged particles that surrounds many planets but had never before been found around a moon.

8

POPULAR DESTINATION

Since 1972, several NASA probes have explored Jupiter and its moons, including Ganymede: Pioneer 10, Pioneer 11, Voyager 1, Voyager 2, Galileo, Cassini-Huygens, New Horizons, and Juno.

9

PRIMITIVE LIFE POSSIBLE

Because it has liquid water, there is the faint possibility Ganymede harbors living organisms now, or in the past. There's no evidence either way at present.

10

WHISTLES A TUNE

Ganymede can be heard whistling and hissing in audio recordings made from plasma wave science data returned by NASA's Galileo spacecraft.
